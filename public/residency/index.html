Invisible College

Creative Residency

a self-guided creative retreat for open source art, design, coding, and cryptocoins

Need a place to get focused creative work done? Want some fun inspiring conversation?
Magic happens when a coder sits next to a designer, a researcher eats dinner with a musician, or a crypto-ronin bunks with a novelist.

The Invisible College is now offering live-in residencies of one week intervals from January 14th through February 28th. There is no fee to participate in the residency. Residents will be reimbursed up to $200 for travel expenses.



Weekly Schedule:


— Sunday afternoon check-in of new residents. Presentation and lunch with current residents. Sunday evening check-out of previous residents.
— Daily morning check-ins on Monday through Saturday

The residency space is a two bedroom loft with abundant sunlight and soaring ceilings. We are located in Bushwick, Brooklyn between the Jefferson L stop and Myrtle-Broadway M stop.

The downstairs private bedroom contains a queen size bed, extremely tall windows, and a large closet. The upstairs shared bedroom contains twin size bunk beds, a closet, and a patio.

The loft is equipped with a fully equipped kitchen, a decadent library with a focus on tech, and plenty of workspace for creation and collaboration.

We are close to lots of cheap late night eats like Archies, Taqueria Sofia, Green Street Salads, and City Fresh Market.

Well renowned and/or experienced creatives may apply for the private bedroom. Creatives in the earlier stages of their career may apply for one of the two bunks in the shared room.

To apply, please send answers to the followings questions to residency@invisible.college

  • 1. What one or two week project (new or existing) would you like to work on at the Invisible College? Be specific and concrete.
  • 2. Imagine you're writing a blog post to tell the world about your work, displayed on Invisible College website. What would it describe? What photos / videos / sound recordings / interactive demos would it have?
  • 3. Imagine you're sharing your work with fellow residents at the end of each week and speaking for about 10 minutes. What would you say / present / demo?
  • 4. Who's a master in your field that you admire and why?
  • 5. Tell us about your past work. We love links to your social media and web presences: e.g, artists/designers => instagram, writers => blog, coders => github / demo links, musicians => soundcloud/bandcamp.
  • 6. Where would you present this work after leaving Invisible College? (Meetup, conference, performance, publication). (p.s. we love receiving postcards from residents)

Thank you for your interest, and best of luck!
